10fixes in this report
High priority Fix first

1. The site does not work on phones

What we saw: opened on a phone sized screen, every page loads the full desktop layout. Guests must pinch, zoom and scroll sideways to read anything.

Why it costs bookings: most hostel guests search and book from their phones, and Google also ranks sites lower when they are not mobile friendly.

The fix: rebuild the pages so they reshape to any screen. This is exactly what the free preview shows.

2. The Rates page shows prices from 2023

What we saw: the Rates page header still reads "From 01.03.2023 till 31.05.2023", checked today, more than three years out of date, with the price table sitting under it.

Why it costs bookings: a visitor reads that the prices expired in May 2023 and concludes the site is abandoned, so they stop trusting anything on it.

The fix: remove the fixed date window and show current prices, or pull them from your live availability.

High priority, continued Fix first

3. The map on the Location page is broken

What we saw: the page made for directions shows a gray box with Google's own error message, "This page can't load Google Maps correctly." No working map appears; only the long written directions below it.

Why it costs bookings: guests trying to find a house in the Old Town's lanes hit an error at the exact moment they are planning how to reach you, and they leave for Google instead. The fix: reconnect the map (a small Google setting) or switch to a map service that needs no key at all.

Medium priority Worth fixing soon

4. "Check availability" sends your guests away to Booking.com

What we saw: the "Check availability" button on every page leaves hosteloldplovdiv.com entirely and lands on a Booking.com widget with its own branding and its own room photos. It even has a small display bug: the currency menu shows a broken code instead of the euro symbol next to "Euro".

Why it costs bookings: the guest leaves your site at the most important moment, books through a platform that charges you commission, and you never get their email address for future direct stays. The fix: a booking page on your own site, which is the second half of the free preview we built for you.

5. The Guest Reviews page has no reviews on it

What we saw: the page titled "Guest Reviews" shows only a heading, a "write a review" link, a list of press links and a row of logos. Not a single guest quote appears.

Why it costs bookings: reviews are your strongest asset, yet the page built for them is empty, which reads worse than having no page at all. Your best reviews live on your Booking.com, TripAdvisor and Hostelworld profiles, not on your own site.

The fix: show a handful of real quotes from those profiles, with the guest's name and date.

6. Broken page title, no description for Google

What we saw: the browser tab and the Google result show "| Hostel Old Plovdiv", a stray vertical bar with nothing before it. There is also no short site description, so Google picks random text for your search listing.

Why it costs bookings: your Google result looks broken before anyone even clicks, which lowers clicks from search.

The fix: a proper title and a two line description for each page. Small job, quick win.

7. The About page is a decade old and contradicts your own prices

What we saw: the Hostel page calls the building a "144-year old Renaissance house". Built in 1868, that maths was true around 2012. The same page says beds start "from 10 EUR", while your Rates page lists the cheapest bed at 15 EUR.

Why it costs bookings: a guest who reads 10 EUR and then sees 15 EUR feels misled right before paying.

The fix: say "built in 1868", which is always true, and match the starting price to the Rates page.

8. A translation tool is installed, but guests cannot switch language

What we saw: the site carries a very old translation add-on, yet no language switcher appears anywhere, and the Bulgarian address of the site returns an error page.

Why it costs bookings: the site is English only in practice, while Old Town Plovdiv welcomes plenty of guests who would rather read in their own language.

The fix: remove the dead add-on and, if you want, add working language versions properly.

9. The text uses the browser's plain default font

What we saw: the site loads no font of its own, so everything renders in plain Times New Roman, the browser fallback.

Why it costs bookings: a property with restored antique beds and a Roman wall in the dining room ends up looking like an unstyled document online.

The fix: one warm, readable font family across the site.

10. Award badges from 2014 and 2015 for a program that ended

What we saw: every page shows TripAdvisor "Certificate of Excellence" badges for 2014 and 2015. TripAdvisor closed that program in 2018.

Why it costs bookings: outdated awards quietly tell visitors nobody has touched the site in years.

The fix: replace them with your current ratings and the newer Travelers' Choice recognition.

One thing worth keeping exactly as it is: your Trips page. Eight detailed Rhodope day trips, Bachkovo Monastery, Perperikon, the Wonderful Bridges and more, each with clear per person pricing. Most hostel sites have nothing like it. In the preview we carried this content over as a highlight, not an afterthought.
Where to start

Ordered by impact. Items 2, 3, 5 and 6 are small jobs any web person can do this week; items 1 and 4 are the rebuild the preview shows.

  1. Make the site work on phones (finding 1). Everything else matters less if mobile visitors leave first.
  2. Remove the 2023 dates from the Rates page and match the About page price (findings 2 and 7).
  3. Fix the broken map on the Location page (finding 3).
  4. Take bookings on your own site instead of handing every guest to Booking.com (finding 4).
  5. Put real guest quotes from your OTA profiles on the Guest Reviews page (finding 5).
  6. Fix the page titles and add descriptions so your Google listing looks right (finding 6).
  7. Then the polish: a proper font, current badges, and working language versions if you want them (findings 8 to 10).
